Books for adults

The Hungry Mind

by Susan Engel

English

Author: Susan Engel

Title: The Hungry Mind

Publisher: Harvard University Press, Boston, MA, USA

Year: 2016

'The Hungry Mind' is an investigation into childhood curiosity: its origins in babies, its development through the early explorations of toddlers, and its flowering in the persistent questioning that comes with the child's mastery over language. Weaving together her decade-long research with her own memories of childhood and experiences of schooling, Susan Engel draws out themes that illuminate the rocky path taken by curiosity over the years. She argues for the power of curiosity to drive learning. Most importantly, she brings out the critical role that adults play, often unconsciously, in either encouraging or suppressing the natural curiosity of children.

Available in: English

Why? What Makes Us Curious

by Simon & Schuster

English

Author: Simon & Schuster

Title: Why? What Makes Us Curious

Publisher: Simon & Schuster, New York, NY, USA

Year: 2017

Curious about curiosity, an astrophysicist delves into the literature in psychology and neuroscience, to understand theories of curiosity since 1960s, the later experiments using fMRI methods, and then conjectures about the evolutionary origins of curiosity. Mario Livio prefaces the review with impressions of Leonardo da Vinci and Richard Feynman, two of the most curious geniuses in history. He draws parallels between research on curiosity, his case studies and learnings from his conversations with some curious people.

Available in: English

The Philosophy of Childhood

by Gareth B. Matthews

English

Author: Gareth B. Matthews

Title: The Philosophy of Childhood

Publisher: Harvard University Press, Cambridge, MA, USA

Year: 1994

The innocent comments and questions of children are often surprisingly perceptive and thought-provoking. Gareth Matthews shows how children's naive questions can lead to a critical, inquiring dialog and how their insights can recall for us the arguments of ancient philosophers. He illustrates how successful children's literature, while simple in style, carries philosophical depth. Matthews critiques contemporary models and theories of childhood, including Piaget's theory of cognitive development and Kohlberg's theory of moral development. Addressing issues like the rights of children, their conception of death and child art, he shows the problematics of evaluating children by any standard measures of ‘maturity’.

Available in: English

The Having of Wonderful Ideas

by Eleanor Duckworth

English

Author: Eleanor Duckworth

Title: The Having of Wonderful Ideas: And Other Essays on Teaching and Learning

Publisher: Teachers' College Press; Third edition

Year: 2006

The microcosm of the classroom holds varied possibilities for invention and exploration. In this collection of essays Eleanor Duckworth recounts from her experience some creative intellectual acts of elementary school children and teachers. She shows how Jean Piaget's ideas help us to understand and enable a rich repertoire of actions and thoughts: involving making connections, predicting and drawing inferences. Such deep engagement enables children and teachers in these stories to experience the fulfilment of constructing their own knowledge.

Available in: English

The Hundred Languages of Children

(Editors) Carolyn P. Edwards, Lella Gandini, George Forman

English

Editors: Carolyn P. Edwards, Lella Gandini, George Forman

Title: The Hundred Languages of Children: The Reggio Emilia Experience in Transformation

Publisher: Praeger, Connecticut, USA. Third Edition

Year: 2011

This is a volume of articles, essays and interviews about the origins, philosophical underpinnings, evolution of educational practice and other aspects of the much acclaimed system of early childhood education in the Reggio Emilia region of northern Italy. Reggio Emilia’s preschools have long attracted researchers and education practitioners from the world over, for their innovative methods rooted in relationships between children, teachers and the community. The title of this book derives from a fundamental principle at the heart of the Reggio Emilia educational practice - namely, respect and acknowledgement of the varied multi-dimensional perspectives and sense-making of children, and a dignified documentation of the same.

Available in: English
