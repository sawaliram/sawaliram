{% trans '...and Sawaliram was born!' %}

{% trans 'It was 1972 and a group of scientists, university academics, graduate students and school teachers' %} {% trans 'were gathering in the village of Rasulia, in the Hoshangabad District of Madhya Pradesh State.' %} {% trans 'They wished to develop an active, inquiring and experiment-based science program, ' %} {% trans 'a curriculum that was rooted in rural India.' %} {% trans "Their efforts led to the landmark 'Hoshangabad Science Teaching Program" %} (HSTP)' {%trans ', one that was implemented in the next 30 years over large tracts of the State.' %}

{% trans "HSTP and its "%} {% trans "'Bal Vaigyanik'" %} {% trans "('Child Scientist' in Hindi) textbooks offered children many opportunities to observe, experiment" %} {% trans "and investigate their surroundings. As a result children came up with a host of intriguing" %} {% trans "questions which they and their teachers naturally lacked the resources to handle." %} {% trans "Thus it was that 'Sawaliram' was born. The year was 1978." %}

{% trans "Over the decades that followed tens of thousands of children sent postcards and letters to" %} {% trans "Sawaliram and were rewarded with personalised replies by volunteers." %} {% trans "A few hundreds of these questions and answers were published in magazines." %}

{% trans "The trend of responding to children's questions caught on in other places. In Mumbai" %} {% trans ", at the Homi Bhabha Centre for Science Education, the 'Shanka-samadhan' ('Curiosity satisfied')" %} {% trans "program addressed questions mailed in by children. In Kerala a group of scientists, engineers and" %} {% trans "writers got together in the 1960s with an aim to develop scientific and technical literature in" %} {% trans "Indian languages. By the start of the 1970s the 'Kerala Shastra Sahitya Parishad (KSSP)' was" %} {% trans "publishing magazines for children and responding to their questions. KSSP's ideal of a 'Peopleâ€™s " %} {% trans "Science Movement' was adopted by popular groups in other States of India, leading in 1988 to the" %} {% trans " All India People's Science Network" %} {% trans ", in which many groups have run their own Q&A programs in different Indian languages." %}

{% trans "The magic of children's questions has touched many, among them a few well-known Indian scientists" %} {% trans " who took it upon themselves to call for children's questions and answer them through " %} {% trans "personal letters. Today many science institutes, universities and colleges conduct outreach " %} {% trans "programs with Q&A sessions. In classrooms as well, students may ask questions." %}

{% trans "The Sawaliram website is built as a repository for all of these questions which are being " %} {% trans "continually asked by children over the years. Sawaliram provides a home where children's " %} {% trans "questions can be submitted, viewed, answered, queried and analysed. Significantly these " %} {% trans "questions give us all: parents, educators, curriculum makers, writers and researchers, " %} {% trans "a glimpse into the rich and multifaceted world of the child." %}
